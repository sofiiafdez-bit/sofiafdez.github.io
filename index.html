I am drawn to psychology because of what it asks us to do: to look carefully at human beings and resist easy explanations. What interests me most is not behaviour as a surface phenomenon, but the processes underneath — how people regulate emotion, construct meaning in relationships, make sense of themselves and others, and navigate a social world that was not always designed with them in mind. I am particularly interested in women, whose inner lives have historically been misread, pathologised, or simply not studied on their own terms.

My research sits at the intersection of mentalization theory, trauma-informed practice, and transdiagnostic assessment. I am interested in what happens when women present with complex socioemotional difficulties that do not fit neatly into a single diagnostic category — and that require clinical formulation attentive to neurodevelopment, attachment, trauma history, and the function of symptoms over time. I believe that more careful science in this area leads to more humane clinical care.

In Press

Fernández Medina, S., Vidal Gutiérrez, J., & Duarte Díaz, A. (in press)

Advances in Autism, Emerald Publishing

Systematic review examining the evidence base for Mentalization-Based Treatment targeting social cognition and emotion regulation across BPD and ASD in adults. Accepted and awaiting final editorial revisions.

Full text available upon publication. This review synthesises evidence for MBT as an intervention addressing shared clinical dimensions across borderline personality disorder and autism spectrum disorder in adult populations, with a focus on emotion regulation and social cognition as transdiagnostic targets.
